## Purpose

This document defines the core strategic frameworks the Content Strategist applies when building content pillar architecture, COPE decomposition plans, topical authority assessments, and thought leadership programs for B2B SaaS.

---

## 1. Content Pillar Architecture

### Definition
A content pillar is a broad, ICP-relevant topic domain supported by a comprehensive pillar page and 8–12 cluster posts. Each cluster post targets a long-tail keyword within the pillar domain and links back to the pillar page, signaling topical authority to search engines.

### Pillar Selection Criteria (all three must be met)
1. ICP pain anchor confirmed: the pillar topic maps to a documented ICP pain point — sourced from GTM.md, sales call transcripts, or support ticket themes
2. Search demand validated: primary keyword has ≥ 500 monthly searches with keyword difficulty ≤ 60 (source: SEO Manager using Semrush/Ahrefs)
3. Coverage gap exists: no existing Conclave content or only thin coverage (< 3 posts) on this topic

### Pillar-Cluster Relationship
- Pillar page: comprehensive guide to the topic (2,000–4,000 words); targets the head keyword; links out to all cluster posts
- Cluster post: targets a specific long-tail keyword within the pillar; 800–2,000 words; always links back to the pillar page
- Internal linking is bidirectional: pillar → clusters and clusters → pillar

### Topical Authority Threshold
- A pillar is considered authoritative at ≥ 8 live cluster posts + a complete pillar page
- No new pillar is added until at least one active pillar reaches this threshold
- Below threshold: production resources stay concentrated within existing pillars

### Pillar Count Target
- Pre-seed / Seed: 1–2 active pillars maximum (capacity constraint)
- Series A: 2–3 active pillars
- Series B+: 3–5 active pillars

## 2. COPE Framework (Create Once, Publish Everywhere)

### Definition
COPE is a content distribution methodology where every long-form content asset (blog post, white paper, case study) is treated as a source document and decomposed into derivative formats before publication.

### COPE Decomposition Protocol
For every long-form piece (≥ 1,500 words), the following derivatives are planned at brief stage:

| Derivative | Volume | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn posts (stat pull, insight extract, or POV argument) | 3 per piece | Social Media Manager |
| Newsletter section (summary + key takeaway) | 1 per piece | Email Marketing Manager |
| Social graphic (quote card or stat infographic) | 1–2 per piece | Art Director / Social Media Designer |
| Podcast talking point | 1 per piece (if podcast is active) | Podcast host / Content Strategist |
| Short-form video hook (if video is active) | 1 per piece | Video Editor |

### COPE Timing Rule
Decomposition plan is completed at brief stage — before the source piece is written. Retrofitting decomposition after publication creates alignment gaps between the source argument and the derivatives.

## 3. Content Type Matrix

### Mapping Content Type to Funnel Stage and ICP Awareness Stage

| Content Type | Funnel Stage | ICP Awareness Stage | Primary CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| "What is X" educational blog post | TOFU | Unaware / Problem-Aware | Subscribe / Download |
| Trend / industry analysis | TOFU | Problem-Aware | Subscribe / Share |
| Thought leadership / POV | TOFU | Problem-Aware | Follow / Subscribe |
| Comparison guide ("X vs. Y") | MOFU | Solution-Aware | Request demo / Start trial |
| Case study | MOFU | Solution-Aware / Product-Aware | Request demo |
| ROI calculator / template | MOFU | Solution-Aware | Request demo |
| White paper / technical deep-dive | MOFU | Solution-Aware | Download (gated) |
| "Why [product]" product-focused post | BOFU | Product-Aware | Free trial / Book a call |
| Implementation guide | BOFU | Most Aware | Onboarding / Expand |

### 50/30/20 Content Distribution Rule
- 50% Educational / Informational (TOFU-dominant, builds organic reach and ICP awareness)
- 30% POV / Thought Leadership (drives brand authority and newsletter growth)
- 20% Commercial / Product-Aware (BOFU — directly supports pipeline)

Applied per sprint, not per piece. A sprint can have a single BOFU piece as long as the quarter balances to 50/30/20.

## 4. Thought Leadership Content Architecture

### Executive POV Content Program
Maps founder or executive POV content to ICP awareness stages:
- Problem-Aware stage: posts that name and frame the problem the ICP faces (no product mention)
- Solution-Aware stage: posts that present a distinctive POV on how the problem should be solved (positions the company's approach, not the product)
- Product-Aware stage: posts that demonstrate outcomes (case study references, data from real customers)

### Ghostwriting Protocol
Content Strategist produces the brief and angle; Performance Copywriter drafts the text. Executive reviews and approves. Minimum cadence: 2 POV posts per week on LinkedIn (founder account), 1 byline per quarter in a domain-relevant publication.

### LinkedIn Thought Leadership Format Selection
| Format | Best for | Engagement pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Single text post | Strong POV / contrarian take | High shares if angle is novel |
| Document / carousel | Step-by-step frameworks | High saves |
| Article | Deep-dive arguments | Low immediate reach, high evergreen value |
| Video clip | Behind-the-scenes / culture | High reach, lower intent |

## 5. ICP Awareness Stage Classification (Schwartz Model)

The Eugene Schwartz five-stage awareness model applied to B2B SaaS content:

| Stage | Definition | Content that matches | CTA type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Doesn't know they have the problem | Educational / trend content | Soft CTA: share / subscribe |
| Problem-Aware | Knows the pain, doesn't know solutions exist | "Why X happens" content, problem framing | Subscribe to newsletter |
| Solution-Aware | Knows solutions exist, comparing options | Comparison guides, case studies, ROI calculators | Demo / trial |
| Product-Aware | Knows about the product, hasn't committed | Testimonials, product deep-dives, pricing clarity | Book a call / free trial |
| Most Aware | Ready to buy, needs a reason | Promotions, guarantees, onboarding proof | Sign up / start trial |

Every content brief must specify the ICP awareness stage before production begins.

## 6. Key Benchmarks (B2B SaaS)

| Metric | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Time-on-page (long-form blog, 1,500+ words) | ≥ 3:30 minutes |
| Scroll depth (long-form) | ≥ 60% |
| Email newsletter click rate | 2–5% (industry median: 2.1%) |
| Content-sourced MQL conversion rate | 1–3% of organic sessions |
| LinkedIn organic reach per post (< 10K followers) | 500–2,000 impressions |
| Organic traffic to MQL conversion | 0.5–2% (B2B SaaS median) |
