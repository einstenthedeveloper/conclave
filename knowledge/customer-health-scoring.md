# Customer Health Scoring
> Applies to: Customer Success Manager, Onboarding Specialist, Director of Customer Success, VP Customer Success, RevOps Manager
> Status: stub
> Created: 2026-05-01 by HR agent

---

## Scope

This document covers how account health should be modeled, inspected, and acted on in a Conclave post-sale motion. It defines factor categories, weighting logic, thresholds, risk CTAs, and the review discipline required to prevent "surprise churn."

---

## Health Factor Categories

When populated, this doc should define which signals contribute to health, for example:

- product adoption / usage
- onboarding completion
- support ticket volume / severity
- stakeholder sentiment
- executive sponsor continuity
- value realization evidence
- renewal posture

Gainsight and ChurnZero both make the same core point: one signal alone is not enough.

---

## Segment-Specific Profiles

This doc should define when different customer segments need different health models:

- SMB vs enterprise
- self-serve vs high-touch
- single-product vs multi-product
- early onboarding vs mature account

One universal score is usually too blunt.

---

## Thresholds and CTAs

When populated, define:

- healthy / watch / at-risk thresholds
- which trigger creates a CTA
- who owns each recovery action
- expected response time by severity

A score without a response model is just a dashboard decoration.

---

## Risk Review Discipline

This section should define:

- weekly review cadence
- required review questions
- how root cause is recorded
- when leadership escalation is required

Default rule:
- Every red or rapidly declining account must have a named owner and next action.

---

## Health Score Pitfalls

When populated, include guardrails against:

- usage-only scoring
- stale manual sentiment fields
- equal weighting for unequal risks
- no segment differentiation
- health updates without action ownership

These are common reasons teams think they have health scoring when they only have health coloring.

This doc should eventually support:

- weekly health review tables
