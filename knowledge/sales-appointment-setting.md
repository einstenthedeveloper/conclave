## Scope

This document covers the operational mechanics of converting warm interest into attended sales meetings. It defines speed-to-lead discipline, qualification-lite rules, scheduling-friction reduction, reminder cadence, no-show recovery, and the metrics that distinguish good booking from vanity booking.

---

## Speed-to-Lead Discipline

When populated, this doc should define:

- response-time SLA by source
- owner assignment rules
- escalation rules when a queue breaches SLA
- after-hours and overflow handling

Salesloft's 433-company survey used five minutes as the critical benchmark and found only 7% of companies responded inside it. HubSpot's current lead-response guidance still stresses sub-30-minute follow-up as a meaningful efficiency and conversion advantage.

Rule:
- A warm lead waiting in queue is a revenue risk, not a neutral backlog item.

## Qualification-Lite for Booking

This role is not full discovery, but it is not blind scheduling either.

When populated, define:

- minimum fit checks before a meeting can be booked
- which BANT fields are required at booking stage
- which conditions force reroute to nurture or disqualification
- what notes must be handed to the closer

Key principle:
- If the meeting would obviously waste AE time, it should not be booked.

## Disarm - Purpose - Question

HubSpot's appointment-setting guidance cites this structure from AA-ISP sales leadership training:

- **Disarm**: lower resistance and acknowledge interruption
- **Purpose**: say why the call exists
- **Question**: ask a simple qualifying question that advances the call

This should be the default opening framework for short booking calls and live response.

## Scheduling Friction Reduction

When populated, include:

- live scheduler use
- timezone-safe defaults
- availability-window design

- reschedule handling

Calendly customer evidence shows reduced scheduling friction can materially shorten time-to-meeting and improve conversion.

## Confirmation and Reminder Workflow

This doc should define the default reminder cadence for sales meetings.

Baseline recommendation from Calendly's sales guidance:

- immediate confirmation after booking
- 24-hour reminder with purpose / context
- day-of reminder or reconfirmation
- post-no-show reschedule workflow

Calendly reports surveyed sales users decreased no-shows by 28% using automated reminders, and customer case studies show larger reductions.

## No-Show Recovery

When populated, define:

- what counts as a no-show
- how long to wait before recovery outreach
- when to reschedule vs when to downgrade or recycle
- how no-show reasons are logged

No-shows should be treated as diagnosable system failures:

- weak reminder flow
- wrong slot timing
- weak meeting value framing
- low-fit booking
- slow response after initial interest

---

## Booking Metrics

This doc should standardize:

- response SLA attainment
- meeting booked rate
- show rate
- no-show rate
- reschedule rate
- meeting-to-opportunity conversion
- low-fit rejection rate by closer

The point is to separate:

- activity
- booked volume
- attended volume
- downstream pipeline quality

Those are not interchangeable.
