# Performance Copy Hooks
> Domain: Conversion Copywriting | Applies to: Performance Copywriter, Traffic Manager, Performance Creative
> Status: stub | Created: 2026-04-25

---

## Purpose

Hook selection determines whether a reader stops scrolling and reads the first line — or keeps moving. This document covers: (1) the 30+ hook archetypes organized by type, (2) hook selection criteria by platform and audience awareness stage, (3) scroll-stop rate and open rate benchmarks, and (4) the two-part hook structure for ads (visual hook + text hook) and email (subject line + preview text).

---

## What a Hook Is — and Is Not

A hook is not a headline. A hook is the first moment of contact that creates enough pattern interruption or emotional resonance to produce a pause. On Meta Ads, the hook is the visual (first 2 seconds of video or the image) PLUS the first line of text. On email, the hook is the subject line PLUS the preview text. On landing pages, the hook is the H1 PLUS the subheadline. All three are two-part systems — treating one element as the hook and the other as decoration is a documented conversion failure mode.

A hook that "explains the product" is not a hook — it is a description. Descriptions do not stop scrolls; they confirm what the reader already assumed. A hook creates a gap (curiosity, fear, identity challenge, unexpected claim) that the reader wants to close by reading further.

## Hook Archetypes (30+)

### Group 1 — Curiosity Gap
Archetypes that create an information gap the reader wants to close.

1. **The Withheld Mechanism**: "Most [ICP] [achieve X outcome], but they never talk about the one thing that makes it actually work."
2. **The Counterintuitive Claim**: "The reason your [X] isn't working is not what you think."
3. **The Secret Pattern**: "[N] out of [M] [ICP] who [achieve X] do this before [expected action]."
4. **The Forbidden Knowledge**: "What [authority figure / competitor / industry] doesn't want you to know about [topic]."
5. **The Question Unanswered**: "What would happen if you never [tedious task] again?"

### Group 2 — Fear of Loss / Pain Amplification
Archetypes that activate loss aversion — Kahneman's prospect theory: losses are felt ~2.5× stronger than equivalent gains.

6. **The Cost of Inaction**: "Every day you [don't do X], [bad outcome accumulates]."
7. **The Missed Window**: "The [opportunity] window closes [timeframe]. Here's where most [ICP] end up after missing it."
8. **The Hidden Drain**: "You're losing [$X / N hours / competitive position] right now because of [problem]."
9. **The Status Threat**: "While you're [common activity], your competitors are [gaining ground on X]."
10. **The Comparison Trap**: "[ICP role] who don't [do X] [outcome that signals professional inadequacy]."

### Group 3 — Direct Benefit / Outcome-First
Archetypes that lead with the desired result. Most effective for Product-Aware and Most-Aware audiences.

11. **The Specific Outcome**: "[Exact metric or result] in [timeframe], without [common objection]."
12. **The Before/After Bridge**: "Before: [current painful state]. After: [desired state]. The bridge: [product/method]."
13. **The Shortcut**: "Skip [tedious process]. [Desired outcome] in [fraction of expected time]."
14. **The New Method**: "The new way [ICP] are [achieving outcome] without [old painful method]."
15. **The Permission**: "You don't have to [sacrifice] to [achieve X] anymore."

### Group 4 — Social Proof / Credibility Opener
Archetypes that lead with evidence before the claim.

16. **The Number Proof**: "[N] [ICP role] [achieved X] with [method/product]."
17. **The Named Authority**: "[Recognized name] said this is the [superlative] way to [achieve X]."
18. **The Testimonial Hook**: "'[Exact customer quote about a specific outcome]' — [Name, Title, Company]."
19. **The Trend Signal**: "[Platform / media] is calling [approach] the [year] standard for [outcome]."
20. **The Category Leader**: "The [descriptor] used by [recognizable companies or ICP peer group]."

### Group 5 — Identity / Tribe
Archetypes that trigger social belonging or self-concept. Most effective when ICP has strong in-group identity.

21. **The In-Group Signal**: "This is for [ICP who meets specific criteria] — and no one else."
22. **The Values Mirror**: "If you believe [shared value], you already understand why [product/approach] exists."
23. **The Identity Statement**: "[ICP who achieves X] are different from [ICP who doesn't]. Here's how."
24. **The Aspiration Ladder**: "The [ICP] who [current state] becomes [desired identity] by changing one thing."
25. **The Rejection Signal**: "Not for everyone. If you [specific trait], this probably isn't for you."

### Group 6 — Narrative / Story Opener
Archetypes that pull the reader into a story before the product is mentioned.

26. **The Moment of Failure**: "I lost [X] because I didn't know [lesson]. Here's what I learned."
27. **The Turning Point**: "Six months ago I was [problem state]. This is what changed."
28. **The Found Discovery**: "I stumbled on [insight] while [relatable activity]. I've never [done X the old way] since."
29. **The Unexpected Journey**: "I was [skeptic position] until [specific event] changed everything."
30. **The Dramatic Result Tease**: "The campaign that [specific result]. Here's exactly what we did."

### Group 7 — Contrarian / Pattern Interrupt
Archetypes that challenge assumed wisdom — high risk, high reward.

31. **The Myth Debunk**: "Everyone says [conventional wisdom]. It's wrong."
32. **The Industry Critique**: "The [industry] has been telling you [claim]. Here's why they're wrong."
33. **The Self-Critique**: "We used to recommend [approach]. We don't anymore. Here's why."
34. **The Reversal**: "Less [expected thing]. More [unexpected thing]. Better [outcome]."

## Hook Selection by Platform

| Platform | Hook format | Primary hook element | Secondary hook element | Best archetypes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Meta Ads (feed) | Visual + first line of primary text | Image or video first 2s | First line of text copy | Pain amplification, curiosity gap, social proof, direct benefit |
| Meta Ads (stories/reels) | First 2–3 seconds of video | Visual motion / text on screen | Voiceover or caption | Identity, direct benefit, narrative opener |
| Google Search Ads | Headline 1 (30 chars) + Headline 2 | Headline 1 | Headline 2 adds specificity | Direct benefit, specific outcome, problem statement |
| Email subject line | Subject line + preview text | Subject line | Preview text completes or extends the hook | Curiosity gap, direct benefit, fear of loss, social proof |
| LinkedIn sponsored | First line of text before "see more" | First 1–2 sentences | Image if used | Credibility-first, direct benefit, contrarian |
| Landing page H1 | H1 + subheadline | H1 (6–10 words) | Subheadline (1–2 sentences, adds mechanism) | Direct benefit, outcome-first, specific claim |

## Hook Selection by Awareness Stage (Eugene Schwartz)

| Awareness stage | Audience knows | Best hook archetypes | What to lead with |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Neither the problem nor the solution | Narrative opener, curiosity gap, identity signal | A story or emotion — not a product or problem name |
| Problem-Aware | The problem exists, not that solutions exist | Pain amplification, fear of loss, myth debunk | The pain — validate it, name it specifically |
| Solution-Aware | Solutions exist, not your specific one | Contrarian, direct benefit, method superiority | Your approach vs. alternatives — not product name |
| Product-Aware | Your product exists, not fully convinced | Social proof, specific outcome, objection handling | Evidence — proof, numbers, testimonials |
| Most Aware | Ready to buy, needs a reason to act now | Direct benefit, permission, urgency | The offer, the guarantee, or a scarcity signal |

## Scroll-Stop Rate Benchmarks

> Note: benchmarks vary by industry, audience quality, and bidding strategy. These are directional reference points, not pass/fail thresholds.

| Platform | Metric | Weak | Acceptable | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Meta Ads video | 3-second view rate (scroll-stop proxy) | < 10% | 15–25% | > 30% |
| Meta Ads static | CTR (link click) | < 0.5% | 1–2% | > 3% |
| Email | Open rate (subject line proxy) | < 15% | 20–35% | > 40% |
| Email | Click-to-open rate (body copy + CTA) | < 5% | 8–15% | > 20% |
| Landing page | Time-on-page > 60s (hook held attention) | < 30% of sessions | 40–55% | > 60% |

## Two-Part Hook Protocol

For every ad and email, document both hook elements before delivery:

```
Ad Hook Record:
- Visual hook: [description of image/video first frame + intended emotion]
- Text hook (first line): [exact text]
- Hook archetype: [named archetype]
- Awareness stage targeted: [stage]
- Hypothesis: [what this tests vs. alternative]
- Backup variant: [alternative text hook for A/B test]

Email Hook Record:
- Subject line: [text] — character count: [N]
- Preview text: [text — extends subject line, does not repeat it]
- Hook archetype: [named archetype]
- Awareness stage targeted: [stage]
- Variant subject line B: [alternative for A/B test]
- Predicted open rate range: [based on benchmark + audience quality]
```

## Common Hook Failures

1. **The Double Hook (two messages fighting)**: "Save time AND make more money AND reduce stress with [Product]." Three hooks in one headline = no hook. Reader cannot identify the single gap to close. Fix: one hook, one gap, one CTA.

2. **The Clever Headline Nobody Gets**: Wordplay, puns, or cultural references that require context the reader may not have. Clever loses to clear at every awareness stage below Most Aware. Fix: test clear vs. clever — clear wins most of the time for cold audiences.

3. **The Feature Disguised as a Hook**: "New dashboard with real-time analytics available." This is a product update, not a hook. No gap is created, no emotion is activated. Fix: translate to outcome — "See which deals will close before your Monday call" (curiosity gap + direct benefit).

4. **The Curiosity Bait-and-Switch**: Hook promises a revelation or secret; body copy delivers a generic product pitch. Reader feels deceived; trust drops. Evidence: CopyHackers' "3 Essential Copywriting Techniques" — curiosity works only when the reveal delivers on the promise. Fix: curiosity hooks require a reveal that is proportionate to the gap created.
