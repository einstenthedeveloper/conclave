## Scope

This document covers the operating system of post-sale customer success in a Conclave environment. It defines how success plans are structured, how lifecycle playbooks are triggered, how executive reviews should prove value, how renewal readiness is prepared, and how expansion signals should be surfaced without confusing success work with commercial ownership.

---

## Success Plan Architecture

When populated, this doc should define:

- customer outcome fields
- milestone structure
- owner assignment rules
- dependency tracking
- review cadence

Core principle:
- A success plan is the post-sale translation of the sold promise into measurable work.

---

## Lifecycle Playbooks

This doc should eventually define standard playbooks for:

- new-customer onboarding
- adoption recovery
- sponsor change
- health decline
- renewal preparation
- expansion signal surfacing

Planhat's lifecycle playbook model is useful here: each playbook needs a trigger, objective, ordered steps, ownership, and completion criteria.

## Executive / Business Reviews

When populated, define:

- what qualifies as an EBR / QBR
- required inputs
- outcome proof structure
- stakeholder expectations
- next-period action format

Key rule:
- The review must show business change, not just product activity.

## Renewal Readiness

This section should define:

- 120 / 90 / 60 / 30-day renewal checkpoints
- value-proof requirements
- risk-review expectations
- sponsor alignment checks
- commercial handoff timing

Lincoln Murphy's retention-vs-renewal distinction matters here: keeping a customer engaged and formally securing a renewal are related but not identical jobs.

---

## Expansion Signal Handling

When populated, define:

- what counts as an expansion signal
- when CS should escalate to sales or leadership
- what evidence is required before surfacing a CSQL
- how to avoid premature upsell pressure

Expansion should emerge from value and need, not from quota anxiety.

## Voice of Customer Loop

This doc should define:

- request and friction capture fields
- routing to product, support, and leadership
- urgency taxonomy
- monthly synthesis format

The goal is to preserve customer truth in a reusable operating format instead of leaving it in scattered notes.

## Core Metrics

When populated, track:

- GRR
- NRR
- logo churn
- time-to-value
- onboarding completion rate
- success-plan coverage
- EBR / QBR completion rate
- risk recovery rate

If these are absent, CS work becomes anecdotal rather than inspectable.
