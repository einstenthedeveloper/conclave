---

## Scope

This document covers how Conclave should translate revenue goals into executable ownership structures. It defines segmentation taxonomy, coverage-model design, capacity assumptions, book construction, quota / target allocation logic, and review rules for rebalancing territories or account books.

---

## Segmentation Taxonomy

When populated, this doc should define:

- approved segments (e.g. SMB, mid-market, enterprise, vertical, geography, product motion)
- the fields used to assign a record to a segment
- who can change segment logic
- how segment exceptions are handled

Coverage design starts with segmentation clarity, not rep preference.

---

## Coverage Model Design

This section should define:

- which roles cover which segment
- named-account vs round-robin rules
- inbound vs outbound ownership logic
- when pooled queues are allowed

Core rule:
- coverage design must protect customer response speed and pipeline quality, not just appearance of fairness.

---

## Capacity Modeling

When populated, define:

- account / lead load assumptions per owner
- response-SLA constraints
- pipeline-capacity assumptions
- meeting / opportunity throughput assumptions
- when headcount or coverage changes are required

Capacity models should explain what the team can realistically absorb before quality drops.

---

## Book Construction and Balancing

This doc should define:

- how books are initially assigned
- how strategic accounts are carved out
- rebalance triggers
- protected accounts or exception cases

Balanced books are not just even record counts. They must account for effort, opportunity quality, and expected support load.

---

## Quota / Target Allocation

When populated, define:

- target-setting inputs
- how quotas or output targets roll down by segment / role / region
- top-down vs bottom-up reconciliation rules
- documentation required before a target change takes effect

Target allocation should be traceable to assumptions, not retroactive pressure.

---

## Review Cadence and Exceptions

This section should define:

- quarterly planning review cadence
- emergency rebalance criteria
- acceptable exception classes
- who signs off on territory or target changes

If exceptions are frequent and undocumented, the coverage model is not stable enough to trust.
