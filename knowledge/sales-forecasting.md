## Scope

This document covers forecast discipline for active opportunities in a Conclave revenue motion. It defines forecast categories, evidence thresholds, slippage interpretation, review cadence, and what must be true before a deal can be treated as commit-quality.

---

## Forecast Categories

When populated, define standard meanings for:

- Pipeline
- Best Case
- Commit
- Closed

Each category should be tied to evidence, not rep confidence.

Potential evidence dimensions:

- Decision-maker engaged
- Champion quality
- Buyer-owned next steps
- MAP health
- Paper process known
- Commercial alignment
- Signature path timing

---

## Forecast Inspection Questions

This doc should standardize the weekly inspection questions, for example:

- Why does this deal close in this period?
- What buyer event or commitment proves that?
- Who can kill the deal and have we engaged them?
- What can still slip the date?
- Which milestone is buyer-owned versus seller-owned?
- If this is `commit`, what evidence would downgrade it?

---

## Slippage Taxonomy

When populated, include:

- Technical slippage
- Procurement slippage
- Legal slippage
- Qualification slippage
- Stakeholder slippage
- Commercial slippage
- AE execution slippage

Every slipped deal should be classified, not vaguely described as "pushed out."

---

## Push / Pull Logic

This doc should define:

- What justifies pulling a deal into a period
- What forces a push-out
- Which conditions convert `best case` to `commit`
- Which conditions force removal from forecast

Forecasting is not about optimism; it is about evidence-weighted timing.
