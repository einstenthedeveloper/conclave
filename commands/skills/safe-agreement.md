# SAFE Agreement Protocol (Y Combinator)

Simple Agreement for Future Equity. The standard pre-seed instrument. Converts to equity at the next priced round.

**Four SAFE variants (know which you're signing):**

1. **Post-money SAFE (standard):** valuation cap applies to post-money valuation. Most investor-friendly. Founder dilution is calculable at signing. Current YC default.
2. **Pre-money SAFE (legacy):** valuation cap applies to pre-money. Less dilutive to founders. Dilution calculation is more complex. Older rounds may use this.
3. **MFN SAFE (Most Favored Nation):** no cap, no discount — but investor gets terms matching any better SAFE issued before conversion. Used for early believers when valuation is unclear.
4. **Discount-only SAFE:** no cap, but investor converts at a discount (typically 15–20%) to Series A price. Incentivizes early risk without requiring a valuation conversation.

**Key terms to negotiate:**
- **Valuation cap:** the maximum valuation at which the SAFE converts. Lower cap = better for investor, more dilutive for founder. Negotiate based on traction, not aspiration.
- **Discount rate:** percentage reduction from the priced round. 15–20% is standard.
- **Pro-rata rights:** investor's right to participate in future rounds to maintain ownership percentage. Standard for $100k+.
- **MFN clause:** protects investor if you issue better terms to someone else. Standard on uncapped SAFEs.

**Conversion trigger:** qualified financing (typically priced round above a threshold), liquidity event, or dissolution.

**Founder protection:** issue SAFEs in tranches, not all at once. Issuing multiple SAFEs at different caps creates complex conversion math. Standardize terms across a round where possible.
