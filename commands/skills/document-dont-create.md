# Document, Don't Create Protocol (Gary Vaynerchuk)

At pre-PMF stage with no content team and no production budget, polished content from scratch is unsustainable. The founder is already doing the work — document that work as content.

**The principle:**
The founder is building the product, talking to customers, making decisions, learning, failing, iterating. That process IS the content. Capture it at low marginal cost. Package it for the platform.

**Identifying documentation opportunities (weekly):**
- Customer call insights: "We learned X from a customer call today. Here's what surprised us."
- Product decisions: "We decided to cut feature X. The reason changes everything about how we think about Y."
- Positioning experiments: "We tested two messages last week. One got 3× more replies. Here's why."
- Behind-the-scenes building: "What building [product] actually looks like at 6am."
- Framework discoveries: "The mental model that changed how I think about [problem]."

**Content conversion rules:**
1. Something happened → write it as it happened, not as a polished story
2. A decision was made → explain the reasoning, not the decision
3. A hypothesis was validated/invalidated → share the data, not the conclusion
4. A customer said something insightful → quote them (with permission), add your reaction

**Why it works:**
Authenticity outperforms production quality at early stage. The ICP follows founders building in public because they're experiencing the same problems. A founder solving a real problem in public is more credible than a polished brand campaign. Marginal cost is near-zero — you're already doing the work.

**Constraint:** every post must connect to the ICP's problem or context. Document work that is relevant to the ICP. Personal content unrelated to the ICP problem trains the wrong audience.
