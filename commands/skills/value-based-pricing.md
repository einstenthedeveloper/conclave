# Value-Based Pricing Protocol (ProfitWell / Patrick Campbell)

Cost-plus pricing is wrong at early stage. The right anchor is the value delivered to the customer, not the cost to deliver it.

**The 1% rule (ProfitWell research):**
1% improvement in monetization → 13% improvement in bottom line (vs. 7% from acquisition and 3% from retention). Pricing is the highest-leverage growth lever. Treat it as a product decision, not an afterthought.

**Value metric selection:**
The pricing metric should scale with the value the customer receives.
- Seat-based: value is per-user productivity. Works when individuals make the buying decision.
- Usage-based: value is per-outcome. Works when consumption directly correlates with benefit.
- Outcome-based: value is per-result. Works when result is measurable and attributable.
- Flat: value is access. Works when the product has a clear utility threshold and usage is unpredictable.

**Willingness to pay (WTP) research:**
Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter — ask 4 questions to 20+ target customers:
1. At what price is this too cheap to trust?
2. At what price is this a bargain?
3. At what price is this starting to get expensive but still worth it?
4. At what price is this too expensive?

Plot distribution. Acceptable price range = intersection of "too cheap" rising curve and "too expensive" falling curve.

**Anchoring tiers:**
3-tier structure: entry (captures price-sensitive segment), core (captures primary ICP — this is where 70% of revenue comes from), enterprise (captures high-willingness-to-pay segment, sets reference price that makes core feel reasonable).

**Pre-PMF pricing rule:**
Charge from day one. A customer who will not pay is not a customer — they are a user who will give you misleading retention data. Charge what you would be embarrassed to charge, then double it.
