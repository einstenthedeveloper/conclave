# LTV:CAC Acquisition Gate

No paid channel scales before this gate is validated. Applying it prematurely to organic or founder-led channels is a category error — it applies to paid acquisition only.

**LTV (Lifetime Value):**
LTV = Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA) × Gross Margin % × Average Customer Lifetime (months)

If churn is unknown: use 12 months as conservative baseline for pre-PMF models.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost — full cost):**
CAC = (Ad spend + agency/tools fees + sales salaries allocated to new business + onboarding cost) / New customers acquired

Common error: calculating CAC as ad spend only. Blended CAC including all people and tools costs is the number that matters.

**Gate thresholds:**
- LTV:CAC < 1 → losing money on every customer. Stop. Fix pricing or reduce CAC before spending another dollar.
- LTV:CAC 1–2 → marginal. Not scalable. Optimize one variable before increasing spend.
- LTV:CAC ≥ 3 → gate passed. Channel is eligible for scaling investment.
- LTV:CAC ≥ 5 → strong signal. Prioritize this channel.

**Payback period:**
CAC / (ARPA × Gross Margin%) = months to recover CAC.
Target: ≤ 12 months for SaaS. > 18 months is a cash flow problem at scale.

**When CAC is unknown (pre-launch):**
Set a CAC ceiling: "We will not scale this channel if CAC exceeds $X." Define X from LTV before spending, not after.
