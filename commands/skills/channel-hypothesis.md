# Channel Hypothesis Execution Protocol

A channel hypothesis is not a plan — it is a falsifiable test with a binary outcome.

**Hypothesis format:**
"[Channel] will produce [N leads / signups / qualified conversations] at a cost below [$X] within [30] days."

Every field is required. "We'll test LinkedIn" is not a hypothesis. "LinkedIn outreach will produce 10 qualified conversations with [ICP role] at < $50 CAC within 30 days" is.

**Channel eligibility criteria (before testing):**
1. ICP is active on this channel (evidence-based, not assumed)
2. Budget or time commitment required is sustainable for 30 days without results
3. Measurement is possible — you can track leads/signups/conversations specifically from this channel
4. Team has execution capacity — who will actually run this test, daily?

**Execution sequence:**
Week 1: Setup + baseline (create account, establish presence, run first N attempts)
Week 2–3: Core test (daily execution at minimum viable frequency)
Week 4: Measure + decide (hit threshold = validated; miss threshold = invalidated)

**Invalidation is not failure:**
A channel that doesn't meet the threshold in 30 days has done its job — it saved 6 months of wasted budget. Document: what was tested, what the result was, what the hypothesis was, why it was invalidated. Move to the next candidate channel.

**Channel stacking failure mode:**
Running 5+ channels simultaneously at pre-PMF. No channel gets enough signal to validate. No learning accumulates. Budget fragments. Avoid by committing to one primary channel until validated or invalidated.

**Validation threshold:**
Not "some results" — a specific number. If threshold is ambiguous, the test cannot be evaluated. Set the threshold before starting. Change it only if the underlying market condition changes, not because the test is failing.
