# Equity Vesting Protocol

Standard structure for co-founder and early employee equity. Prevents cap table disasters from early departures.

**Standard vesting schedule: 4-year vest / 1-year cliff**
- 25% vests at month 12 (the cliff). If person leaves before month 12, they receive 0%.
- Remaining 75% vests monthly over months 13–48 (1/36th per month after cliff).
- Total vest: 48 months (4 years).
- No acceleration by default. Acceleration requires explicit negotiation.

**Why the cliff matters:**
The cliff is a mutual commitment test. If the co-founder or employee leaves before 12 months, the working relationship was not viable — equity should not vest for work the company didn't benefit from. Without a cliff, a co-founder can leave at month 3 with 6.25% of the company.

**Acceleration provisions (negotiate explicitly):**
- Single trigger: full acceleration on acquisition. Protects employees from being acquired and immediately let go. Acquirers dislike this because it removes retention incentive.
- Double trigger: acceleration only if (1) acquisition AND (2) employee is terminated without cause within 12–24 months. Balanced. Standard for key hires.

**Co-founder vesting:**
If company existed before co-founder joined, backdate cliff to founding date or to when co-founder started contributing substantially. Document in board resolution. All co-founders must be on a vesting schedule — no exceptions. Unvestable equity held by departed co-founders is a cap table red flag that blocks fundraising.

**Early employee options (ISO/NSO):**
- Grant date: as early as possible (lower 409A valuation = lower strike price).
- Exercise window: standard 90 days post-termination. Negotiate 5–10 years for key hires.
- 83(b) election: file within 30 days of restricted stock grant. Locks in tax basis at grant date.
