# JTBD Interview Protocol (Christensen / Bob Moesta)

Customers hire products to do a job they cannot do with current tools. The interview extracts the "struggling moment" — the specific event that triggered search for something new.

**Minimum viable interview (5 sessions, 30 min each):**

**Opening:** "Walk me through the last time you [did the thing this product does]. Start from the beginning — what was happening that day?"

**Extract in order:**
1. **First thought** — "When did you first realize you needed something different?" (anchors the trigger)
2. **Passive looking** — "What did you look at first? Why there?" (reveals consideration set)
3. **Active looking** — "What made you decide to seriously evaluate options?" (identifies urgency moment)
4. **Decision** — "What made you choose [product/alternative]?" (reveals decision criteria)
5. **First use** — "What happened the first time you used it?" (aha moment vs. expectation gap)
6. **Ongoing use** — "What keeps you using it?" (retention driver vs. lock-in)

**The four forces (apply to every answer):**
- Push: what about the current situation is pushing them away?
- Pull: what about the new solution is pulling them toward it?
- Anxiety: what worries them about switching?
- Inertia: what habits or attachments keep them with the old solution?

**Output:** one paragraph describing the consistent struggling moment across 5 interviews. If no consistency → sample is too heterogeneous → narrow the ICP before messaging.
