# Fogg Behavior Model (B = MAP)

Behavior happens when Motivation, Ability, and Prompt converge at the same moment. Missing any one = behavior does not happen.

**B = M × A × P**

**Motivation (M):** does the user want the outcome right now?
- Sensation (pleasure/pain): is there immediate pleasure or pain at stake?
- Anticipation (hope/fear): is there a near-term outcome they're moving toward or away from?
- Social cohesion (belonging/rejection): does this connect them to or protect them from a social group?
Diagnose: if users aren't taking action, check motivation first. Are they in the right moment? Is the value proposition clear at this point in the flow?

**Ability (A):** can the user do the behavior right now, in this context?
Ability factors: time, money, physical effort, mental effort, social deviance (does it feel weird?), non-routine (does it break a habit?).
Design principle: reduce the behavior to its smallest possible version. The easier the behavior, the less motivation required.

**Prompt (P):** is there a signal to act right now?
Three types:
- Spark: prompts AND provides motivation (use when motivation is low)
- Facilitator: prompts AND reduces friction (use when motivation is high but ability is low)
- Signal: just a reminder (use only when both motivation and ability are already high)

**Conversion design rule:**
Map the conversion funnel as a sequence of B=MAP moments. For each step, identify: Is motivation present? Is the action simple enough? Is there a clear prompt? If a step fails, diagnose which element is missing before redesigning the step.

**Aha moment:** the specific moment when the user first experiences the core value. Everything before the aha moment is friction to minimize. Everything after is retention surface to optimize.
